I am a doctoral candidate and Research Assistant in Curriculum & Instruction at Kent State University, specializing in Early Childhood Mathematics Education.

Explore early childhood mathematics education through innovative research, place-based learning, and community-driven initiatives connecting Nigeria and the United States.

Explore Research

Connect Theory with Practice

I study how young children build mathematical understanding, focusing on sociomathematical norms, fraction division, multiplicative reasoning, common errors in fractions, embodied noticing, and place-based learning approaches that bridge classroom instruction with real-world and community contexts.

Learn About Me
Philosophy ▼

I believe early childhood mathematics education flourishes when research, practice, and community intersect. My philosophy is grounded in the understanding that all children come to school with rich mathematical ideas shaped by culture, environment, and everyday experiences. Through place-based learning, equitable instructional practices, and teacher preparation grounded in noticing children’s mathematical thinking, I am committed to transforming how mathematics is taught and learned in diverse contexts.

Vision ▼

To be a leading scholar in Curriculum & Instruction who advances early childhood mathematics education through culturally responsive practices, place-based pedagogy, interdisciplinary collaboration, and impactful research benefiting communities in Nigeria, the United States, and globally.

Mission ▼

To conduct high-quality research, support teacher development, produce community-embedded STEM initiatives, and mentor future educators. My work aims to enhance mathematics learning by connecting theory to practice and amplifying local knowledge systems that shape children’s mathematical experiences.
