I am a doctoral candidate and Research Assistant in Curriculum & Instruction at Kent State University, specializing in Early Childhood Mathematics Education.

Explore early childhood mathematics education through innovative research, place-based learning, and community-driven initiatives connecting Nigeria and the United States.

Explore Research

Connect Theory with Practice

I study how young children build mathematical understanding, focusing on sociomathematical norms, fraction division, multiplicative reasoning, common errors, and place-based approaches connecting classroom learning to community life.

Learn About Me

Website Philosophy

I believe early childhood mathematics education flourishes when research, practice, and community intersect. This site shares my work connecting classroom learning to real-world contexts, advancing equitable and developmentally appropriate experiences for young learners, and fostering global collaboration to inspire educators and scholars worldwide.

Vision

To be a leading scholar in Curriculum and Instruction, shaping the future of early childhood mathematics education worldwide through innovative research and impactful practice.

Mission

To advance scholarship and improve teaching practices in early childhood mathematics education by conducting rigorous research, mentoring educators, and fostering equitable, developmentally appropriate learning experiences for young children globally.
