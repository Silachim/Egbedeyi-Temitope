Temitope Egbedeyi is a doctoral researcher and Research Assistant in Curriculum & Instruction at Kent State University, specializing in Early Childhood Mathematics Education. Married to Omowumi C. Tope-Egbedeyi, he is passionate about improving how young children learn mathematics through innovative research and practice.

His work focuses on sociomathematical norms, multiplicative reasoning, and eye-tracking technology to better understand student learning. He has authored over 20 publications, led multiple grant-funded projects, and contributed to initiatives like Dreams to Legacy Initiative, Foundation for Rural Education and Empowerment, and The Sanguine Academy.

Recognized in the media for his research, Temitope combines classroom experience in Nigeria with rigorous scholarship to advance meaningful, equity-driven mathematics education for young learners.

Research Interests: Early childhood mathematics, sociomathematical norms, multiplicative reasoning, eye-tracking technology.
