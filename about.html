Temitope Egbedeyi is a doctoral researcher and Research Assistant in Curriculum & Instruction at Kent State University, specializing in Early Childhood Mathematics Education. Married to Omowumi C. Tope-Egbedeyi, I am passionate about improving how young children learn mathematics through innovative research and practice.

My work focuses on sociomathematical norms, multiplicative reasoning, and eye-tracking technology to better understand student learning. I have authored over 20 publications, led multiple grant-funded projects, and contributed to initiatives like Dreams to Legacy Initiative, Foundation for Rural Education and Empowerment, and The Sanguine Academy.

Recognized in the media for my research, I combine classroom experience in Nigeria with rigorous scholarship to advance meaningful, equity-driven mathematics education for young learners.

Research Interests: My research interests include early childhood mathematics, sociomathematical norms, multiplicative reasoning, and eye-tracking technology.
